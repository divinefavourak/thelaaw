ADMINISTRATION OF CRIMINAL JUSTICE ACT (ACJA) 2015

Section 6: Notification of Cause of Arrest
(1) Upon arrest, a suspect must be informed immediately of the reason for the arrest.
(2) Suspects must be informed of their right to remain silent until they have consulted a lawyer.
(3) Suspects are entitled to free legal representation from the Legal Aid Council of Nigeria if they cannot afford one.

Section 7: Prohibition of Arrest in Lieu
(1) It is strictly illegal to arrest a person in place of a suspect (e.g., arresting a relative because the suspect is at large).

Section 8: Humane Treatment
(1) Suspects must be treated with dignity.
(2) Suspects must not be subjected to torture, cruelty, or inhuman/degrading treatment.

Section 10: Inventory of Property
(1) Police must take a signed inventory of all items recovered from a suspect at the time of arrest.

Section 17: Recording of Statements
(1) Statements should ideally be recorded electronically (audio or video).
(2) Written statements must be made in the presence of the suspect's legal practitioner or another person of their choice.

Section 30 & 158: Right to Bail
(1) There is a general entitlement to bail for non-capital offences under reasonable conditions.

Section 33: Monthly Reports of Arrests
(1) Police must submit monthly reports of all arrests (with or without warrant) to the nearest Magistrate.

Section 34: Magisterial Visits
(1) Chief Magistrates are required to conduct monthly inspections of police stations and detention facilities within their jurisdiction to ensure no one is being held unlawfully.

Section 296: Timeline for Detention and Remand
(1) Initial Detention: A suspect must be brought before a court within 24 to 48 hours.
(2) First Remand Order: A court may order remand for up to 14 days if investigation is ongoing.
(3) Extension: Remand can be extended for another 14 days for "good cause."
(4) Final Review: If the suspect is not charged after 28 days, the court summons the authorities to explain the continued detention.
(5) Total Remand Limit: If no progress is made after 56 days, the court may release the suspect.
