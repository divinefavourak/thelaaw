NIGERIA POLICE ACT 2020

Section 32: Prohibition of Arrest for Civil Wrongs
(1) A person cannot be arrested for a civil wrong or a breach of contract (e.g., debt recovery).

Section 34: No Unnecessary Restraint
(1) Handcuffs or physical restraint should only be used if there is a reasonable apprehension of violence, an attempt to escape, or by court order.

Section 35: Notification of Cause of Arrest
(1) An officer must immediately inform the suspect of the reason for the arrest and their right to remain silent or consult a legal practitioner.

Section 36: Prohibition of Arrest in Lieu
(1) It is illegal to arrest a relative, friend, or associate in place of a suspect.

Section 37: Humane Treatment
(1) Suspects must not be subjected to torture, cruelty, or degrading treatment.

Section 49: Stop and Search
(1) Officers can stop and search a person or vehicle if they have "reasonable suspicion" that the person possesses stolen or unlawfully obtained property.

Section 50 & 51: Conduct of Search
(1) Officers must identify themselves (name, rank, and station) before a search.
(2) Searches must be conducted with "strict regard to decency."
(3) A person must only be searched by an officer of the same sex.

Section 54: Prohibition of Profiling
(1) Reasonable suspicion for a search cannot be based on arbitrary factors like age, color, dress, hairstyle (e.g., dreadlocks), or stereotyped images.

Section 60: Right to Counsel during Statement
(1) A suspect has the right to have a legal practitioner, an officer of the Legal Aid Council, or a Justice of the Peace present during the recording of their statement.

Section 62-64: Bail is Free
(1) The Act reinforces that bail for non-capital offences should be granted at the station.
(2) Demanding money for bail is considered extortion and misconduct.

Section 70: Judicial Oversight
(1) A Magistrate must conduct a monthly inspection of police stations and detention facilities to ensure suspects are not being held illegally.
