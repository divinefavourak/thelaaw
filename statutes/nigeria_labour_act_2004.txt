NIGERIAN LABOUR ACT (Cap L1, LFN 2004)

Section 1: Manner of Payment
(1) Wages shall be paid in legal tender (Naira).
(3) Payment in the form of alcohol or at places where alcohol is sold is strictly prohibited.

Section 4: Advances
(1) An employer cannot advance more than one month's wages to a worker.
(2) No interest can be charged on such advances.

Section 5: Deductions
(1) An employer cannot make deductions from a worker's wages unless:
(a) It is a reasonable deduction for injury or loss caused by the worker (with prior written consent from an authorized labour officer).
(b) It is for a contribution to a provident or pension fund agreed to by the worker and approved by the State Authority.
(c) It is for trade union dues (check-off system).

Section 11: Termination of Contracts by Notice
(1) Minimum legal notice depends on length of service:
(a) Less than 3 months: 1 day's notice.
(b) 3 months to less than 2 years: 1 week's notice.
(c) 2 years to less than 5 years: 2 weeks' notice.
(d) 5 years or more: 1 month's notice.
(6) Either party can waive the right to notice by accepting a payment in lieu of notice (salary for the notice period).
(7) All wages due must be paid on or before the expiry of the notice period.

Section 15: Periodicity of Payment
Wages must be paid at the end of the agreed period. If the period is longer than one month, wages must be paid at intervals not exceeding one month.

Section 20: Redundancy
(1) Employer must inform the trade union or workers' representative of involuntary termination due to excess manpower.
(2) The principle of "last in, first out" should generally be applied, subject to merit and skill.
(3) The employer must use their best endeavors to negotiate redundancy payments.
